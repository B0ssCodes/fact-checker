Global Carbon Emissions See Biggest Annual Decline on Record During Pandemic

A new report by the International Energy Agency reveals that global carbon dioxide emissions saw the largest ever annual decline in 2020 due to COVID-19 lockdowns and reduced industrial activity. Emissions dropped by a record 2.4 billion tons, a 5.8% decrease from 2019 levels.

The dramatic dip marks the first time emissions have fallen so steeply in a single year without a decline in economic output. Researchers attribute the historic emissions drop to mass behavior changes as countries restricted travel and manufacturing to control the pandemic's spread.

While emissions are rebounding with economies reopening, experts say the unprecedented decline demonstrates it is possible to rapidly reduce humanity's carbon footprint through sweeping changes to energy use, transportation and industrial output. Some hope the crisis conditions that cut emissions so sharply could inspire more decisive climate action going forward.
