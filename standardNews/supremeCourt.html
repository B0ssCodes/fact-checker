Supreme Court Justice Announces Retirement, Triggering Partisan Battle Over Replacement

Long-serving Supreme Court Justice Stephen Breyer announced this week he will retire, giving President Biden his first opportunity to nominate a justice. At 83, Breyer is the oldest sitting justice and faced pressure from Democrats to step down.

His retirement kicks off a contentious battle in the divided Senate over confirming a successor. Republican lawmakers have signaled they will fight any liberal or moderate nominee. But Democrats aim to move swiftly to hold hearings and fill the vacant seat.

The high-stakes nomination will be consequential for the Court’s ideological balance. Biden pledged to nominate the first Black woman to the Supreme Court while on the campaign trail. With major cases looming, the president now faces heavy pressure to select a progressive replacement who can be confirmed by the Senate’s narrow Democratic majority.
