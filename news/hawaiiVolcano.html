Hawaii Volcano Creates New Island as Lava Flows into Ocean

An ongoing eruption of Hawaii's Kilauea volcano has led to the emergence of a new small island offshore. Rivers of lava from the volcano have been pouring into the Pacific Ocean for months. As the molten rock meets the sea, it rapidly cools and accumulates to form fresh land mass.

The newly birthed island measures approximately one mile wide, but remains highly unstable. Geologists say seismic activity and wave erosion will likely cause the land to change shape or potentially be swallowed by the ocean again. This is not the first time Kilauea has birthed an ephemeral island in recent years.

The Hawaii Volcanoes National Park is monitoring the young island closely. They warn tourists that setting foot on newly formed islands in the area can be extremely hazardous. While the new land mass remains isolated for now, its precarious existence is a reminder of Earth's constantly evolving surface and the raw creative forces still shaping Hawaii's picturesque coasts.
