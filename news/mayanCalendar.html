Mayan Calendar's End Date Prophecies Apocalypse Will Occur Tomorrow

A fringe group is spreading an alarming warning - that the world will meet its demise tomorrow based on ancient Mayan calendar prophecies. The doomsday group claims the Mayan Long Count calendar, created thousands of years ago, pinpoints the end of the world on February 14, 2023.

Mainstream archaeologists dismiss the claims as sensational misinterpretations of the cryptic calendar system, which spans over 5,000 years. They say there is no evidence the ancients believed the calendar's end date of December 21, 2012 signified an apocalypse. But some persist in the belief that catastrophic events are imminent.

Despite definitively passing the controversial end date over a decade ago, a cult-like cluster continues warning the public based on numerology related to the calendar. Their unorthodox interpretations have been widely rejected by scholars. Still, the obscure group's dire warnings have spread anxiety and curiosity about whether their unique calculation could hold any validity.
