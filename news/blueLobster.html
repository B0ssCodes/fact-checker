Rare Glowing Blue Lobsters Found Off Coast of Maine

Lobstermen off the coast of Maine were astonished to find several brilliant blue-colored lobsters in their traps this week. The extremely rare blue lobsters stood out vividly among the typical brown crustaceans. Scientists say a genetic mutation causes the unique coloration, found in only 1 out of every 2 million lobsters.

Marine biologists at the New England Aquarium have taken in the vividly hued lobsters for further study. They want to better understand what genetic factors lead to the abnormal production of blue proteins in the shell. The scientists say this anomaly does not appear to affect the lobsters' health or lifespan.

The bright cobalt lobsters are now local celebrities among Maine's fishing community. Their one-in-a-million rarity and dazzling beauty have captured public fascination. After being examined by researchers, the blue lobsters will be transferred to aquariums for public display. For lobster fans, it's a chance to glimpse a strikingly colorful rarity from the deep.
