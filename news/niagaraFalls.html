Niagara Falls Stuns Tourists By Mysteriously Running Dry

Visitors to Niagara Falls were shocked this week when the iconic waterfalls unexpectedly ran dry overnight. Stunned tourists awoke to find barely any water flowing over the immense cliff, revealing bare and cracked riverbed below.

Authorities are urgently investigating what caused the Niagara river's flow to redirect, shutting down the falls to a mere trickle. Some experts suspect natural adjustments in the riverbed allowed water to reroute away from the ledge. Officials are also checking for artificial diversions of water that could account for the unprecedented event.

While water has returned to the falls, geologists are working to determine if vulnerable points in the river could cause another interruption. The extremely rare phenomenon has also sparked worries about the impact of climate change and water erosion on the falls' future. For now, scientists can only speculate about when or if Canada's most iconic landmark could dramatically run dry again.
