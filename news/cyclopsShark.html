Shark with Strange Cyclops Mutation Discovered in Mexico

Fishermen off the coast of Mexico made a startling discovery this week when they caught a dusky shark with a single giant eye at the center of its head. Marine biologists have confirmed the bizarre shark has a rare mutation called cyclopia, which caused its face to improperly form during embryonic development.

While cyclops mutations occasionally occur in other animals, this is the first known case ever documented in a shark. Researchers are fascinated by the anomalous creature and plan to study it further to understand what caused the genetic abnormality.

The exotic one-eyed shark has become an overnight internet sensation, with many commenting on its uncanny resemblance to a mythical cyclops. While its uniqueness sadly did not allow it to survive long, scientists say examining its remains can provide valuable insights into anatomical development in sharks. The extremely rare specimen will aid future research and be catalogued in the annals of marine biology oddities.
